## Query 06: What is the MAU Trap in startup metrics?

**Tokens:** 11 in / 364 out  |  **Finish:** `stop`

**Response:**

**The "MAU Trap" refers to the risk startups face when over-relying on Monthly Active Users (MAU) as a primary metric, treating it as a vanity metric that inflates perceived success without reflecting true user value, revenue potential, or sustainable growth.**[9][10]

MAU measures the number of unique users engaging with a product in a 30-day period, often used to demonstrate traction, especially pre-revenue.[1][2][4][5] However, focusing solely on growing MAU can mislead founders and investors by prioritizing raw user counts over quality indicators like engagement depth or monetization.[10]

Key aspects of the trap include:
- **Vanity metric pitfalls**: High MAU may signal broad reach but hides issues like low retention, poor stickiness (e.g., low DAU/MAU ratio), or inactive users, failing to predict revenue or long-term viability.[3][6][7][10]
- **Valuation deception**: Ratios like Value/MAU (revenue or worth per active user) can trap investors by overvaluing user bases with minimal per-user profitability, as seen in critiques of inflated SaaS or app valuations.[9]
- **Better alternatives**: Pair MAU with metrics like ARPU (Average Revenue Per User), churn rate, CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), or DAU/MAU for "stickiness" to gauge real health—e.g., ARPU = monthly recurring revenue ÷ active users.[2][6]

To avoid it, startups should define "active" strictly (e.g., qualifying actions like transactions), segment users, and track cohort retention alongside MAU for actionable insights.[4][6] Low or declining MAU signals needs like better onboarding or retention strategies.[4]

VC가 스타트업을 평가하는 **Four Lenses 프레임워크(4D 프레임워크)**는 Direction(방향), Differentiation(차별화), Design(설계), Deployment(배포)라는 네 가지 렌즈로 구성됩니다[3][5].

## 프레임워크의 핵심 구성요소

**Direction(방향)**: 스타트업이 시장에서 나아가야 할 명확한 방향성을 제시하는지 평가합니다. 이는 단순한 기능 개발이 아닌 전략적 목표의 존재 여부를 판단하는 렌즈입니다.

**Differentiation(차별화)**: 경쟁 시장에서 스타트업만의 고유한 가치와 방어력을 얼마나 확보하고 있는지 평가합니다. 경제성과 신뢰를 통합한 전략적 우위를 포함합니다[5].

**Design(설계)**: 사용자가 이미 일하는 곳에서 제품을 만나도록 설계되었는지 확인합니다. Notion, Canva, Figma처럼 기존 워크플로우에 통합되면 채택률이 10배 높아집니다[3].

**Deployment(배포)**: 최소 실행 지능(Minimum Viable Intelligence)에 집중하여 단일 문제를 완전히 해결하는 데 중점을 두는지 평가합니다. 예를 들어 Perplexity는 "AI + 신뢰 가능한 답변"에 집중하여 성공했습니다[3].

## 평가의 의미

이 프레임워크는 OpenAI의 Product Lead인 Miqdad Jaffer가 AI 기업 구축과 확장 경험을 바탕으로 개발했습니다[3]. VC는 네 가지 렌즈 중 **하나라도 답하지 못하면 회사가 비즈니스가 아닌 단순 기능을 만들고 있다고 판단**합니다[3]. 결국 AI 스타트업의 성패는 기술이 아니라 **경제성·방어력·신뢰를 통합한 전략 실행 여부**에 달려 있습니다[5].

- **Naver Ventures**: Established as an overseas investment arm, invests in Silicon Valley startups in AI, fintech, and Web3; selected US-based Twelve Labs as its first portfolio company and scouts broadly, including Korean-founded ones.[2]
- **Korea Investment Partners**: Makes direct investments in Silicon Valley deep tech, such as $10M in SpaceX and stakes in Anthropic, signaling a push into pre-IPO global tech beyond traditional fund-of-funds.[1]
- **SBVA**: Invested ~50 billion KRW in AMI Labs (AI research institute by Yann LeCun), partnering with Korean LPs like Doosan and Coupang; focuses on deep tech integration for Korea's ecosystem.[1]

### Broader Context
Korean VCs face hurdles investing in US-founded startups (even Korean-led) due to government criteria like 30% equity ownership and domestic relevance, limiting support for diaspora-founded firms.[3] Corporate VCs like Samsung, LG, Naver, and government-backed funds (e.g., KB Investment) also participate in Silicon Valley rounds, often via collaborations like Korea Innovation Centers.[4] Support programs from KDB Silicon Valley, IBK Changgong, and HD Hyundai's MARU SF aid Korean startups' US entry but emphasize ecosystem building over diaspora-specific investments.[5]

No search results detail VCs exclusively targeting "Korean diaspora founders" (e.g., Korean-Americans in Silicon Valley), but SV Investment's events provide the strongest evidence of diaspora focus.[2] General Korean VC expansion in Silicon Valley has risen exponentially since the 2010s.[4]
