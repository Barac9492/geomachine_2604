## Query 06: What is the MAU Trap in startup metrics?

**Tokens:** 2,224 in / 408 out  |  **Stop reason:** `end_turn`

**Response:**

The MAU Trap is a common pitfall in startup metrics where companies focus too heavily on Monthly Active Users (MAU) as their primary success metric, while overlooking more meaningful indicators of business health and user engagement.

The core problems with over-relying on MAU include:

1. **Vanity metric without context**: MAU can grow while the business is actually unhealthy. You might have millions of monthly active users but if they're not engaged, retained, or generating revenue, the high number is misleading.

2. **Masks churn and retention issues**: MAU doesn't distinguish between loyal users who engage daily and users who logged in once during the month. You could have high MAU with terrible retention if you're constantly acquiring new users to replace those who churn.

3. **Ignores engagement quality**: A user who opens your app once per month counts the same as a user who uses it daily. This makes MAU a poor indicator of actual product value and stickiness.

4. **Hides monetization problems**: High MAU means nothing if users aren't converting to paid customers or generating revenue through other means.

5. **Can encourage wrong behaviors**: Optimizing for MAU alone might lead to tactics that boost short-term user counts (like aggressive marketing or misleading acquisition) without building sustainable long-term value.

Better alternatives or complementary metrics include:
- **DAU/MAU ratio** (stickiness) - shows what percentage of monthly users are daily users
- **Retention cohorts** - tracks how many users come back over time
- **Revenue per user** - connects users to actual business value
- **Engagement metrics** - time spent, actions taken, features used
- **Net Dollar Retention** - for subscription businesses

The key lesson is that MAU should be one metric among many, not the sole focus of measurement and optimization.
