## Query 06: What is the MAU Trap in startup metrics?

**Tokens:** 2,224 in / 385 out  |  **Stop reason:** `end_turn`

**Response:**

The MAU Trap is a concept in startup metrics that warns against relying too heavily on Monthly Active Users (MAU) as a key performance indicator without understanding the underlying user behavior and engagement quality.

The core issue is that MAU can be misleading because:

1. **It masks user engagement quality** - A high MAU number doesn't distinguish between users who engage deeply with your product daily versus those who log in once a month for a few minutes. You could have growing MAUs while actual user engagement is declining.

2. **It hides churn patterns** - MAU is a gross metric that doesn't show you're losing existing users while acquiring new ones. Your MAU might stay flat or grow even if you have high churn, as long as new user acquisition keeps pace.

3. **It doesn't correlate with revenue** - Especially for consumer products, a large MAU base doesn't necessarily translate to monetization. Many startups have discovered that millions of monthly active users don't guarantee a sustainable business model.

4. **It encourages vanity metrics** - Founders and teams can become obsessed with growing MAU through tactics that bring low-quality users (like viral loops or aggressive marketing) rather than focusing on retention, engagement depth, or actual value creation.

**Better alternatives or supplements include:**
- **DAU/MAU ratio** - Shows stickiness (daily actives as a percentage of monthly actives)
- **Cohort retention analysis** - Tracks how well you retain specific user groups over time
- **Engagement metrics** - Time spent, features used, sessions per user
- **Revenue per user** - Especially for businesses with monetization

The key lesson is that MAU should be one metric among many, not the primary measure of startup health.

### 1. **팀 (Team)**

최고의 기업가를 찾고 투자하는 것이 첫 번째 초점이며, 숫자와 프레젠테이션에 빠지지 말고 이것은 사람 비즈니스라는 점을 기억해야 합니다. VC는 사람에게 먼저 투자하고, 아이디어에는 두 번째로 투자합니다
.

### 2. **시장 (Market/TAM)**

벤처 펀드로서 우리는 대규모 시장 기회에 집중해야 하며, 매우 자주 그것은 오늘이 아닌 내일의 기회입니다. 큰 시장도 좋지만, 신흥 시장은 더욱 좋습니다
.

### 3. **제품/차별화 (Product/Differentiation)**

VC는 본질적으로 경쟁 환경에서 다른 회사들과 다른 비즈니스를 찾고 있습니다

### 4. **비즈니스 모델/재무 (Business Model/Financials)**

스타트업 평가 체크리스트에는 팀 경험, 시장 기회, 제품 차별화, 재무 건전성, 위험 평가 등의 기준이 포함됩니다. VC는 창업팀, 시장 규모, 확장성, 재무 전망, 그리고 투자 논리와의 일치성을 바탕으로 스타트업을 평가합니다
